## System Overview

The Queue Management System is designed to make your service experience efficient and organized. Instead of waiting in long lines, you can generate a queue number and wait comfortably until your number is called.

### Key Benefits
- **No Physical Lines**: Wait comfortably instead of standing in line
- **Fair Service**: First-come, first-served with priority for special categories
- **Real-time Updates**: See your queue status on display screens
- **Audio Announcements**: Hear when your number is called
- **Estimated Wait Times**: Know approximately how long you'll wait

## Getting Started

### Step 1: Locate the Kiosk
Find the public kiosk terminal in the service area. It's typically a touchscreen device or computer with the queue system interface.

### Step 2: Choose Your Service
Select the service you need from the available options:
- Cashier Services
- Information/Inquiries
- Documentation Processing
- Technical Support
- Customer Service
- And other available services

### Step 3: Select Priority Category (If Applicable)
Choose your priority category if you qualify:
- **Normal**: Standard service priority
- **Senior Citizen**: 60+ years old
- **Person with Disabilities**: PWD card holders
- **Pregnant Women**: With medical certificate if needed
- **Priority**: Other special cases as defined by the office

### Step 4: Get Your Queue Number
Click "Get Queue Number" to receive your ticket. The system will:
- Generate a unique queue number
- Show your number on screen
- Display estimated wait time
- Print your ticket (if printer is available)

1. **Select Service**: Tap on your desired service
2. **Choose Priority**: Select appropriate category
3. **Confirm**: Review your selection and confirm
4. **Receive Number**: Note your queue number

### Important Notes
- Take a photo or write down your queue number
- Keep your ticket if printed
- Don't lose your number - you'll need it to know when you're called
- The system prevents duplicate numbers for the same service

## Understanding Priority Categories

### Priority System
The system serves customers based on priority to ensure fair and efficient service:

#### Priority Levels (Highest to Lowest)
1. **Priority** (Level 9)
2. **Person with Disabilities** (Level 8)
3. **Pregnant Women** (Level 7)
4. **Senior Citizens** (Level 6)
5. **Normal** (Level 0)

### How Priority Works
- Higher priority numbers are served first within the same service
- If you have priority, you may be served before people who arrived earlier
- Priority categories are verified at the service window
- False priority selection may result in being asked to rejoin the normal queue

### Eligibility Requirements
- **Senior Citizen**: Must be 60+ years old (ID may be required)
- **PWD**: Must have valid PWD identification
- **Pregnant**: Must be visibly pregnant or have medical certificate
- **Priority**: Special cases as defined by the service provider

## Reading Queue Displays

### Public Display Board
The main display screen shows:

#### Now Serving Section
- Currently being served queue numbers
- Window numbers where service is happening
- Service types being provided

#### Waiting Queue Section
- Next 5-10 numbers in queue
- Service types for each number
- Estimated wait times

#### Information Section
- Current time
- Office announcements
- System status

### Understanding Display Information

**Queue Number Format**
- Prefix + Number (e.g., CSH001, INF015)
- Prefix indicates service type
- Number is your position in sequence

**Window Numbers**
- Each service window has a unique number
- Your ticket will show which window to go to
- Multiple windows may serve the same service

**Status Indicators**
- **Now Serving**: Currently being assisted
- **Waiting**: Your turn is coming up
- **Completed**: Service finished

## What to Expect When Called

### Audio Announcements
When your number is called:
- **Audio**: "Now serving number [Your Number] at window [Window Number]"
- **Visual**: Your number appears on the "Now Serving" display
- **Repetition**: Announcement repeats 2-3 times

### When to Go to Window
- Proceed to the assigned window immediately
- Have your documents ready
- Bring your queue ticket if printed
- Arrive within 2 minutes of being called

### At the Service Window
1. **Present Your Number**: Show your queue number
2. **Verify Priority**: Show ID if you selected priority category
3. **State Your Request**: Clearly explain what you need
4. **Follow Instructions**: Listen to the service provider's guidance

### If You Miss Your Call
- Your number may be called again
- If you miss multiple calls, you may need to get a new number
- Inform the service provider if you need assistance

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: How long will I have to wait?**
A: Wait times vary based on:
- Number of people ahead of you
- Service complexity
- Number of available windows
- Staff availability

The system provides estimated wait times when you get your number.

**Q: Can I change my service selection after getting a number?**
A: No, you'll need to get a new queue number for the correct service.

**Q: What if I lose my queue number?**
A: Check the display board for your number, or ask staff for assistance.

**Q: Can someone else get a number for me?**
A: Yes, but you must be present when called for service.

### Priority Questions

**Q: What if I don't have ID for priority category?**
A: You may be served as normal priority. Bring ID next time.

**Q: Can I select multiple priority categories?**
A: No, select only one applicable category.

**Q: What if my priority isn't honored?**
A: Inform the service provider or speak with a supervisor.

### Technical Questions

**Q: What if the kiosk isn't working?**
A: Approach staff for manual queue number assignment.

**Q: Can I use my phone to get a queue number?**
A: Some offices offer mobile queueing - check with staff.

**Q: What if the display screen isn't updating?**
A: Inform staff immediately - there may be a technical issue.

### Service Questions

**Q: Can I transfer to a different service?**
A: You'll need a new queue number for the different service.

**Q: What if I need multiple services?**
A: Get separate queue numbers for each service needed.

**Q: Can I make an appointment?**
A: This system is for walk-in service only. Check with office for appointments.
